+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| FORMATTING GUIDELINES                                                                                      |
+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+

Keep all tabs within 110 characters for readability.

Use the line below as a width guide.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Lyrics Guidelines:
https://community.musixmatch.com/guidelines

- Format lyrics according to the song structure. Keep each section to a maximum of 10 lines.
- Break lines where the vocal naturally pauses. Each line should match the performance.
- Use sentence case. Capitalise only the first letter of each line and proper nouns.
- Do not use end-line punctuation, except for question marks.
- Write numbers as words from one to ten. Use numerals for 11 and above, dates and specific times.
- Use standardised spelling for slang and abbreviations (e.g. ’cause, tryna, I’ma).
- Include all repeated lyrics in full. Do not use x2, x3, etc.
- Do not include section labels (e.g. Verse, Chorus) within the lyrics.
- Use brackets for backing vocals or non-primary lines.
- Format direct speech using quotation marks, with a comma before the quote and a capital letter to begin.


Tab Guidelines:
https://tabs.ultimate-guitar.com/contribution/help/rubric

- Use standard notation symbols consistently (see legend below).
- Include 'Time Signature' only if not 4/4. Place directly under BPM.
- Use standard short chord notation (e.g. E7sus4, Cmaj7, D/F#). Use * to indicate a variation.
- Sequential chords (no lyrics): Write chords on one line, spaced evenly across the bar with two spaces between each chord.
- No chord (N.C.): Use when no harmony is played. Place where chords would normally appear.
- Repeats: Use (x2), (x3) etc. after a sequence, with one space before the repeat marker.
- Spoken word: Use brackets before the vocal to indicate spoken or non-sung lines.

Rhythm Guide:

Basic Beats (4/4)
  .   .   .   .
|-1---2---3---4---|

Quavers (8th Notes)
  . & . & . & . &
|-1-&-2-&-3-&-4-&-|

Semiquavers (16th Notes)
  . e & a . e & a . e & a . e & a
|-1-e-&-a-2-e-&-a-3-e-&-a-4-e-&-a-|

Triplets (3 per beat)
  . t t . t t . t t . t t
|-1-t-t-2-t-t-3-t-t-4-t-t-|

Mixed Subdivisions (expand only where needed per beat)
  . & . e & a . e & a . & t & t &
|-1-&-2-e-&-a-3-e-&-a-4-&-t-&-t-&-|

Rules:

- The dot (.) marks the main beat and is visible in the final tab.
- Add subdivisions only where required.
- Align notes directly under the subdivision they are played on.
- Subdivision labels can be mixed within a section if timing is clear.
- Subdivisions can be expanded within a beat where needed (e.g. quavers + triplets).
- Subdivision markers (e, &, a, t) are for spacing only and are not written in the final tab.


Notation Legend:

h = hammer-on
p = pull-off
/ = slide up
\ = slide down
s = slide
b = bend
pb = Pre-bend
br = Bend release
pbr = Pre-bend release
brb = Bend release bend 
r = reverse bend, or release bend
t = right-hand tap
x = muted/dampened string
~ or v = vibrato
<5> or + = harmonic
P.M. = palm muting
(5) = ghost note, or optional note

Strumming Legend:

D  - Down strum  
U  - Up strum  
B  - Bass-only strum (lowest-pitched string)  
d  - Soft down strum (gently strum bass strings)  
x  - Choked or muted strum  
>  - Accent (place before D, U, d or B – e.g. >D, >U, >d or >B)
